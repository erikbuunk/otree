As announced, for each correct answer to a general knowledge question, you will be paid a piece rate of least {{Constants.low_pr}} GBP. To receive this piece rate, it does not matter whether you submitted a correct answer right away (in Part 1) or after you have sought advice (in Part 2).
All that matters to obtain this piece rate is that you ultimately submitted the correct answer to a question.

For seeking advice there is an advice fee of {{Constants.advice_cost}} GBP per question. This fee will be deduced from your payment if you chose the advice option even if you ended up not changing your initial answer. If you proceed to the next question without seeking advice, you do not pay the fee.

Seeking advice can also affect the piece rate for correct answers in the following way:

The piece rate for correct answers is at least {{Constants.low_pr}} GBP but can also be higher: {{Constants.high_pr}} GBP per correct answer. The computer determines whether you receive the low piece rate of {{Constants.low_pr}} GBP or the high piece rate of {{Constants.high_pr}} GBP per correct answer as follows:

If you pass on the option to seek advice for all 10 questions, it will be more likely that you will be paid the high piece rate than the low piece rate. Specifically, 8 out of 10 times the computer will select the high piece rate and 2 out of 10 times, the computer will select the low piece rate. In other words, the chance of getting the high piece rate will be 80%.

If, instead, you seek advice on at least one question, it is equally likely that you will be paid the high piece rate or the low piece rate. Specifically, 5 out of 10 times, the computer will select the high piece rate and 5 out of 10 times, the computer will select the low piece rate. In other words, the chance of getting the high piece rate will be 50%.
