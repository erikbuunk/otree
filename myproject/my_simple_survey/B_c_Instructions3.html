Your Estimate

For each of the 10 questions, you provide your estimate by entering an integer number between 0 and 100 into the statement: “I am __ % certain that my answer is correct.”. Higher numbers indicate that you assign your answer a higher a chance of being correct.

Payment Rule

At the end of the study one of your 10 estimates will be randomly selected and evaluated for payment. The selected estimate influences your chance of earning {{Constants.confidence_pay}} GBP from this part of the study.

*** The payment rule is designed such that you have the largest chance of earning additional {{Constants.confidence_pay}} GBP by reporting your most accurate guess of the likelihood that your chosen answer is correct.***

You will be awarded additional {{Constants.confidence_pay}} GBP if the selected answer to the question is correct. Yet, for this part either your answer or the answer of a special computer program will be used.

Computer Program

The program selects the correct answer with a certain likelihood that can be as low as “certainly the wrong answer is selected” (0 % likelihood) or as high as “certainly the correct answer is selected” (100 % likelihood) or any value in between 0, 1, 2, 3, …., 97, 98, 99, 100%. Each of these 101 levels of accuracy of the computer program is equally likely and therefore there is only a 1/101 chance that the computer will select the correct answer with certainty.

The estimate you enter determines how accurate the computer program is supposed to be to be allowed to replace your answer. As you only want the computer program to replace your answer if it is more likely to submit a correct answer than yourself, you should enter your best estimate of how likely your own answer is to be correct. That is, reporting your most accurate guess of the chance that your chosen answer is correct secures you the largest chance of earning an additional {{Constants.confidence_pay}} GBP bonus.

If you would like to know more about the exact payment rule, this information is available at the end of the study.

In other words, if you think that the chance is low that you have submitted the correct answer, you should indicate this by entering a low number in the box, if you want to secure a larger chance of earning {{Constants.confidence_pay}} GBP. If, you think, however, that the chance is medium or high, you should indicate this by entering a medium or high number in the box to secure the largest chance of earning {{Constants.confidence_pay}} GBP from this part of the study.

Further Remarks

Again, this task is timed. You will have {{Constants.confidencetime}} seconds to enter your most accurate estimate of the chance that your chosen answer is correct. If you did not submit an estimate before the timeout and this question was randomly chosen, you would be paid no additional GBP.

What estimate is relevant for the award of the bonus will be determined only after you entered the estimates for all 10 questions. As each question is equally likely to be chosen, you should treat each estimate as the one, that can earn you an additional {{Constants.confidence_pay}} GBP because this may be the case.
